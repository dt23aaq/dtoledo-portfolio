Learning Journeys

Introduction to Data & Database Modelling

Week 1 & 2

During my data & database management course, I gained an understanding of Entity Relationship (ER) modelling in SQL, which is a powerful tool for creating effective and well-organised databases.

ER modelling involves visually representing entities, attributes, and relationships between them.

This technique provides a clearer understanding of complex data structures.

I learned how to apply ER modelling in SQL to design a database schema, and also identify potential data anomalies and inconsistencies that may impact data integrity.

Furthermore, the course equipped me with the skills to use SQL to query and manipulate data based on my ER model.

As a result, I can extract valuable insights and make data-driven decisions. In summary, this course provided me with a strong understanding of ER modelling using SQL, which is an important skill for the future job.

Database Modelling

Week 3

In week 3 in DBMS subject, we were introduced to seveval aspects of SQL, including constraints, functions and groups. I found this subject to be a fascinating one, and I want to gain this skill to benefit me in my future career.

SQL constraints are rules that are applied to a database to ensure that data is entered in a specific format, and they help to maintain the integrity of the database. I have learned about the different types of SQL contstraints, such as primary keys, foreign keys, and unique constraints, and how to use them effectively.

Human Computer Interaction & User Experience

Blog

In today's world, data has become an invaluable resource, and efficient database management is crucial. Recently, I completed a DBMS subject, where I learned the essential concepts and techniques involved in creating, managing, and modifying databases in a professional setting. The course covered a wide range of topics, including data modelling, normalisation, and the use of Structured Query Language (SQL). That’s why I like this subject.



One of the most valuable things I learned in the course was how to use ER modelling in SQL to design effective database schemas. ER modelling involves creating a graphical representation of entities, attributes, and relationships between them. This technique makes it easier to comprehend and manage complex data structures by organising and visualising them. With my newfound knowledge in ER modelling in SQL, I can now design and implement well-structured databases that meet specific business requirements.



Another crucial aspect of the course was learning about data normalisation. This involves organising data in a database to reduce duplication and enhance data integrity. By ensuring that data is consistent and reliable, this technique helps in making accurate business decisions.



Finally, the course introduced me to SQL, the most widely used database manipulation language. I learned how to use SQL to query and manipulate data based on my ER model. This enables me to extract valuable insights and make data-driven decisions. The knowledge and skills I obtained from the course will undoubtedly prove useful in my next data management job. The ability to design effective database schemas, normalise data, and manipulate data using SQL will be invaluable in any data-driven organisation. By staying up-to-date with developments in the industry and continuously developing my DBMS knowledge and abilities, I can have a significant impact on the sector.



In conclusion, database management is an essential and exciting field, and I am eager to continue exploring its many intricacies. The course has given me a solid foundation, and I look forward to building on this foundation through further learning and practical experience. Overall, I believe that the knowledge and skills I gained from the course will help me achieve my professional goals and contribute to the success of any organisation I work for.



The course also taught me the importance of data security and privacy. In today's world, where data breaches and cyber-attacks are becoming more common, it is essential to ensure that sensitive information is protected. I learned about the different methods and techniques used to safeguard databases, such as access controls, encryption, and backups.



Moreover, the course emphasized the significance of database performance optimization. A well-designed database can significantly enhance an organization's productivity and efficiency. I learned about techniques such as indexing, query optimization, and database tuning, which can help improve database performance.



In conclusion, the database management course was a valuable learning experience, and it has equipped me with essential knowledge and skills to work in the field of data management. The ability to design effective database schemas, normalize data, manipulate data using SQL, ensure data security and privacy, and optimize database performance will be invaluable in any data-driven organization. As the world continues to generate massive amounts of data, the demand for skilled professionals in database management will only increase. Therefore, it is crucial to stay updated with the latest developments in the field and continue learning to stay relevant and succeed.
